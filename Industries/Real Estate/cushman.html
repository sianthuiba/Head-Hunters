Cushman & Wakefield

Cushman & Wakefield is a global commercial real estate services company that advises property owners and occupiers on how to buy, sell, finance, lease, manage, and value commercial property. Headquartered in Chicago, it is one of the largest firms in its sector, with tens of thousands of employees and operations across some 60 countries.

Business and Services

Cushman & Wakefield focuses exclusively on commercial real estate rather than residential brokerage in most markets. Core services include property and facilities management, leasing (landlord and tenant representation), capital markets (investment sales and financing), project and development services, and valuation and advisory. For large occupiers, it provides “global occupier services,” helping corporations manage portfolios, workplace strategy, and transactions worldwide.

Scale and Market Position

The company is consistently ranked among the world’s largest commercial real estate services firms, alongside peers such as CBRE, JLL, Colliers, and Newmark. It reported roughly US$9.5–10 billion in annual revenue in the early 2020s and manages billions of square feet of commercial space globally, from office and industrial to retail and specialized assets.

History and Ownership

Founded in New York City in 1917 by J. Clydesdale Cushman and Bernard Wakefield, the firm expanded nationally in the 1960s and internationally from the 1990s onward. It went through several ownership phases (including periods under RCA, The Rockefeller Group, and EXOR) before being acquired by DTZ-backed investors in 2015 and merged with DTZ under the Cushman & Wakefield brand. The combined company later went public on the New York Stock Exchange in 2018.

Recent Notes

Recent coverage has highlighted both expansion moves—such as managing or advising on new office acquisitions in growth markets—and legal scrutiny, including a lawsuit alleging its retirement plan options did not adequately address climate-related financial risks.
